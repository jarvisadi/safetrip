Wildlife Guide for Betla National Park

Tigers
- Active between 5 PM and 7 AM
- Most commonly spotted near Trail 3 and river bank
- Maintain minimum distance of 100 meters
- Never approach or attempt to feed
- If you encounter a tiger: Do not run, back away slowly, make yourself appear larger
- Report sightings immediately to ranger station
- Tiger attacks are rare but can be fatal
- Carry bear spray if permitted

Elephants
- Found near waterhole and forest edges
- Aggressive during monsoon season (June-September)
- Maintain distance of at least 50 meters
- Never come between a mother and calf
- If charged: Stand your ground, make noise, do not run
- Avoid carrying strong-smelling foods
- Elephant herds can be unpredictable
- Stay clear of elephant paths marked with dung

King Cobras
- Found in rocky areas and near fallen logs
- Most active during early morning and late evening
- Venomous but generally avoid humans
- Maintain distance of at least 5 meters
- If encountered: Freeze, do not make sudden movements
- Back away slowly once snake moves away
- Wear closed-toe shoes and long pants
- Check sleeping area before settling in
- Do not attempt to handle any snake

Wolves
- Pack animals, rarely seen by tourists
- Generally avoid human contact
- If encountered: Make yourself appear larger, make noise
- Do not turn your back on wolves
- Maintain distance of at least 30 meters
- Report aggressive behavior to rangers

Sloth Bears
- Found in forested areas, especially near termite mounds
- Can be aggressive if surprised
- Maintain distance of at least 50 meters
- Make noise while walking to avoid surprising them
- Never approach cubs
- If charged: Stand your ground, make loud noises

Leopards
- Elusive, rarely seen during day
- Active at night and early morning
- Found in rocky terrain and dense forest
- Maintain distance of at least 50 meters
- Store food securely to avoid attracting them
- Report any unusual behavior

General Wildlife Safety
- Never feed any wild animal
- Do not leave food unattended
- Dispose of waste properly
- Respect wildlife boundaries
- Carry a wildlife guidebook
- Learn to identify animal tracks
- Stay on designated trails
- Do not disturb animals during feeding
- Keep children close at all times
- Carry a whistle for emergencies

Safe Distances from Animals
- Tigers: 100 meters minimum
- Elephants: 50 meters minimum
- Bears: 50 meters minimum
- Leopards: 50 meters minimum
- Wolves: 30 meters minimum
- Snakes: 5 meters minimum
- Deer: 20 meters minimum
- Monkeys: 10 meters minimum

What to Do if Attacked
- Tiger: Fight back with anything available, aim for eyes and nose
- Elephant: Run in zigzag pattern, find cover
- Bear: Play dead if grizzly, fight back if black bear
- Snake: Stay calm, note snake appearance for identification
- Leopard: Fight back aggressively, aim for face
- Wolf: Fight back, protect throat
