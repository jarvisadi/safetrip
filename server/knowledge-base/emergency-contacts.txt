Emergency Procedures
1. Assess the situation calmly
2. Call the most relevant emergency number
3. Provide clear information: location, nature of emergency, number of people
4. Stay on the line until help arrives
5. Follow instructions from emergency responders
6. If possible, send someone to the nearest ranger station
7. Do not leave injured or lost persons alone
8. Mark your location clearly for rescuers

What to Report in Emergency
- Your exact location (trail name, marker number, GPS coordinates if available)
- Nature of emergency (injury, wildlife encounter, lost person, etc.)
- Number of people involved
- Any immediate dangers
- Time of incident
- Contact information of your group

First Aid Kit Essentials
- Bandages and antiseptic
- Pain relievers
- Antihistamines for allergies
- Insect repellent
- Sunscreen
- Blister treatment
- Emergency blanket
- Whistle
- Flashlight with extra batteries

Communication Tips
- Mobile coverage is limited in some areas
- Carry a fully charged power bank
- Know the locations of emergency phones on trails
- Inform someone outside the park of your itinerary
- Check in at regular intervals if possible
- Carry a satellite phone for remote areas
