Trail Safety Guide for Betla National Park

Trail 1 - Easy Trail (Safe Daytime)
- Length: 2 km
- Difficulty: Easy
- Best time: 6 AM to 6 PM
- Features: Flat terrain, well-marked paths, scenic viewpoints
- Suitable for families and beginners
- Rest areas available every 500 meters

Trail 2 - Moderate Trail
- Length: 4 km
- Difficulty: Moderate
- Best time: 7 AM to 5 PM
- Features: Some elevation gain, rocky sections, stream crossings
- Avoid after 6 PM due to reduced visibility
- Carry trekking shoes and water
- Guide recommended for first-time visitors

Trail 3 - Expert Trail
- Length: 8 km
- Difficulty: Expert
- Best time: 6 AM to 4 PM
- Features: Steep climbs, dense forest, remote areas
- Dangerous at night - strictly prohibited after sunset
- Wildlife sightings reported in this area
- Requires prior trekking experience
- Must register at ranger station before entry

General Safety Rules
- Always carry at least 2 liters of water per person
- Inform ranger station before entering any trail
- Stay on marked paths at all times
- Do not leave the trail for any reason
- Carry a whistle and basic first aid kit
- Wear appropriate footwear with good grip
- Check weather conditions before starting
- Never hike alone - minimum 2 people required

Emergency Procedures
- If lost: Stay in place, do not wander
- Blow whistle 3 times in succession every 5 minutes
- Find shelter if weather turns bad
- Conserve water and energy
- Use bright clothing or items to signal
- Wait for rescue - do not attempt to find your own way out
- If injured: Stay calm, apply first aid, use whistle

Trail Markings
- Red arrows: Main trail path
- Blue markers: Water sources
- Yellow signs: Danger zones
- Green posts: Emergency exit points
- White circles: Rest areas

Weather Considerations
- Monsoon season (June-September): Trails may be slippery
- Summer (March-May): Carry extra water
- Winter (November-February): Early morning fog common
- Avoid trails during heavy rain
- Check with rangers for trail conditions
